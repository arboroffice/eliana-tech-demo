You know your business needs AI. You've read the articles, seen the case studies, and maybe even tried a few tools. But between running your business and trying to figure out AI implementation, something isn't clicking.

That's where **done-for-you AI systems** come in. Instead of learning how to build AI infrastructure yourself, you hire experts to design, build, and install it for you. You show up to a business that runs better — without becoming a technologist in the process.

But is it right for you? What does it actually include? And what should you expect to invest? Let's break it all down.

## What "Done for You" Actually Means

A **done-for-you AI system** is a complete, custom-built automation solution installed in your business by an outside team. You're not buying software off the shelf. You're not learning to code. You're hiring specialists to:

1. **Audit your operations** and identify the best AI opportunities
2. **Design custom systems** tailored to your specific workflows
3. **Build and configure** everything — integrations, AI models, automations
4. **Test thoroughly** before anything goes live
5. **Train your team** to use and maintain the systems
6. **Provide documentation** so nothing is a black box
7. **Optimize ongoing** to ensure continued performance

The end result: working AI systems running in your business that your team can operate — without you or the consultant being involved day-to-day.

## Who Done-for-You AI Is For

This approach works best for:

### Business Owners Who Value Time Over Money
You could learn to build AI systems yourself. But the 200+ hours you'd spend learning and experimenting is time you're not spending on revenue-generating activities. If your time is worth $200+/hour, done-for-you pays for itself in the time saved alone.

### Companies Doing $1M-$50M in Revenue
You have enough operational complexity to benefit significantly from AI, enough revenue to justify the investment, and enough at stake that you need it done right the first time.

### Leaders Without Technical Teams
You don't have developers or data scientists on staff. You need someone who can handle the technical side while communicating in business terms.

### Businesses Ready to Scale
You're at a growth inflection point. You need operational infrastructure to handle the next level of revenue without proportionally increasing headcount.

### Owners Who've Tried DIY and Stalled
You signed up for some AI tools, maybe built a few basic automations, but never got the transformative results you expected. You need an expert to connect the dots.

## What a Done-for-You Engagement Looks Like

### Phase 1: Discovery & Audit (1-2 Weeks)

**What happens:**
- Deep-dive interview about your business operations
- Your team is interviewed about their daily workflows
- Current tools and systems are evaluated
- Data flows are mapped
- Bottlenecks and opportunities are identified

**What you receive:**
- AI Opportunity Report with prioritized recommendations
- ROI estimates for each opportunity
- Recommended implementation roadmap
- Technology recommendations

### Phase 2: System Design (1-2 Weeks)

**What happens:**
- Detailed system architecture designed for each recommended automation
- Data flow diagrams created
- Integration points identified
- AI models and prompts designed
- User interface and experience planned
- Testing strategy defined

**What you receive:**
- System design document
- Technical architecture diagrams
- Implementation timeline
- Final budget confirmation

### Phase 3: Build (2-6 Weeks)

**What happens:**
- Integrations built between your existing tools
- AI models configured and fine-tuned
- Automation workflows constructed
- Dashboards and reporting built
- Error handling and fallback systems created
- Internal and external testing

**What you receive:**
- Working systems (in a test environment first)
- Documentation for each system
- Test results and performance data

### Phase 4: Launch (1-2 Weeks)

**What happens:**
- Systems moved from test to production
- Gradual rollout (not everything at once)
- Real-time monitoring during launch period
- Rapid fixes for any issues that surface

**What you receive:**
- Live, running AI systems in your business
- Performance monitoring dashboard
- Emergency contact for issues

### Phase 5: Training & Handoff (1-2 Weeks)

**What happens:**
- Team training sessions (recorded for future hires)
- One-on-one time with key system operators
- Documentation review
- Q&A sessions
- Handoff of system access and credentials

**What you receive:**
- Trained team
- Complete documentation
- Recorded training videos
- Support for a defined period post-launch

### Phase 6: Optimization (Ongoing, Optional)

**What happens:**
- Monthly or quarterly system review
- Performance optimization
- New automation opportunities identified
- System expansion as your business evolves

**What you receive:**
- Optimized systems that improve over time
- Regular performance reports
- A technology partner who knows your business

## What It Costs

Done-for-you AI systems typically fall into these investment ranges:

### Starter Package: $10,000 - $25,000
- 1-3 core automations
- Single department focus (usually sales or support)
- Basic AI integration
- 30-day post-launch support
- Best for: Businesses just starting with AI, revenue $1M-$5M

### Growth Package: $25,000 - $60,000
- 4-7 core automations
- Multi-department coverage
- Advanced AI systems (custom models, complex workflows)
- 60-day post-launch support
- Quarterly optimization included for 6 months
- Best for: Businesses ready to transform operations, revenue $5M-$20M

### Enterprise Package: $60,000 - $150,000+
- Complete operational AI infrastructure
- All departments covered
- Custom AI models and integrations
- Dedicated support for 90 days
- Ongoing optimization retainer
- Best for: Businesses building AI-native operations, revenue $20M-$50M+

### Ongoing Costs
- **AI tool subscriptions:** $200-$2,000/month (depending on volume)
- **Optimization retainer:** $1,500-$5,000/month (optional but recommended)
- **Expansion projects:** Priced individually as needs emerge

## Done-for-You vs. DIY vs. Consulting

### DIY (Do It Yourself)
- **Cost:** $500-$5,000 (tools and subscriptions)
- **Time investment:** 200+ hours of learning and building
- **Risk:** High (you don't know what you don't know)
- **Quality:** Variable (depends on your technical ability)
- **Speed:** 3-12 months to see significant results
- **Best for:** Technical founders with time and patience

### Consulting Only (Strategy + Advice)
- **Cost:** $2,000-$10,000
- **Time investment:** You or your team does the building
- **Risk:** Medium (good strategy, execution varies)
- **Quality:** Depends on your implementation
- **Speed:** 2-6 months
- **Best for:** Businesses with technical teams who need direction

### Done-for-You (Full Service)
- **Cost:** $10,000-$150,000
- **Time investment:** Minimal (you participate in discovery and review)
- **Risk:** Low (experts handle execution)
- **Quality:** High (proven processes and experienced builders)
- **Speed:** 4-12 weeks to live systems
- **Best for:** Business owners who want results without the learning curve

## How to Choose the Right Done-for-You Partner

Ask these questions:

1. **"Can you show me results from businesses similar to mine?"** — Experience in your industry and size matters
2. **"Will I own the systems you build?"** — You should own everything. No lock-in.
3. **"What happens after you're done?"** — Your team should be able to operate without them
4. **"How do you measure success?"** — ROI should be defined upfront
5. **"What does your team look like?"** — You want business strategists AND technical builders
6. **"What's your process for handling something that doesn't work?"** — Things will need adjustment. That's normal.

**Done-for-you AI systems** are for business owners who want the results of AI without spending months becoming AI experts. You invest money instead of time, get expert execution instead of trial and error, and end up with systems that actually work.
