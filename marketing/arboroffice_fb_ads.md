# ArborOffice FB Ads: The 5-Stage Awareness Suite

This document outlines 15 Facebook ad concepts for **ArborOffice**, categorized by Eugene Schwartz's 5 Levels of Awareness. These are designed to move tree service business owners from "overwhelmed climber" to "autonomous operator."

---

## 🏗️ Stage 1: Unaware
*The prospect is "busy" but hasn't realized their business model is the bottleneck.*

### Ad 1: The Ladder Trap
**Headline:** You can't close a $5,000 job from a harness.
**Body Copy:** You're 40 feet up in a White Oak. Your phone is buzzing in your pocket. That's a homeowner ready to sign a contract, but they're calling your competitor because you didn't answer. 
Stop being the bottleneck of your own growth. You don't need another climber; you need a system that closes for you while you're in the tree.

**CTA:** See how it works

### Ad 2: The Linear Scaling Myth
**Headline:** More crews = more chaos?
**Body Copy:** Most tree service owners think the only way to grow is to buy another truck and hire another crew. But more trucks just mean more logistics, more missed calls, and more overhead.
Break the cycle. Scale your revenue without adding headcount. 
**Creative:** Graphic showing "Crew Count" stay flat while "Revenue" goes vertical.
**CTA:** Learn the Strategy

## 📉 Stage 2: Problem Aware
*The prospect knows they are missing money (storms, ghosted quotes).*

### Ad 3: The Storm Surge Leak
**Headline:** How many storm calls did you miss last night?
**Body Copy:** When the wind hits, the calls flood in. 40 simultaneous leads. Your voicemail is full. Your office manager is panicked. Every missed call is $3,000+ walking out the door.
ArborOffice AI handles 50 calls at once, triages by severity, and books the estimates instantly. 
Stop leaking storm revenue.
**Creative:** ![Storm Night](C:\Users\mialo\.gemini\antigravity\brain\ea3e7104-1d2a-46fa-8583-cfd061d6bbe9\arboroffice_storm_night_lightning_1774114835722.png)
**CTA:** Plug the Leaks

### Ad 4: Ghosted Estimates
**Headline:** Your estimates are dying in 'Sent'.
**Body Copy:** You spend 2 hours driving and 1 hour assessing. You send the quote. Then... silence. The homeowner is waiting for 3 other quotes. 
The company that follows up first, most frequently, and most professionally wins. We automate the 45-day follow-up cycle so you don't have to.
**Creative:** Split screen: "Manual Follow-up (0%)" vs "ArborOffice Follow-up (100%)".
**CTA:** Close More Quotes
